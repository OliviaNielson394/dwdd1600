Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence, Jefferson's name is closely associated with the document. He later became the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A well-known diplomat, inventor, and statesman. Franklin was an elder statesman at the time and played a crucial role in gaining French support during the American Revolution.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence, Adams was a key figure in the Continental Congress and would go on to become the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel was a leader of the movement for independence in Massachusetts and a fiery advocate for colonial rights.
John Hancock
Known for his bold and large signature, Hancock was the President of the Continental Congress and a prominent figure in the fight for independence.
Richard Henry Lee
Known for proposing the resolution calling for independence, Lee was an influential Virginian leader and signer.
Charles Carroll
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration, Carroll was from Maryland and would later become a U.S. senator.

These individuals played a vital role in pushing for independence and were central figures in the creation of the new nation. Some of them, like Jefferson and Adams, also had long careers in public service, becoming key architects of the United States.
